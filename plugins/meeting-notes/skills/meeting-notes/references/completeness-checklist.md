# Completeness Checklist

Review the draft against the transcript before presenting to the user.

## Structural check

- [ ] All major discussion topics from the transcript are covered
- [ ] Each topic has attributed points (who said what)
- [ ] Action items have specific individual owners (not "the team")
- [ ] Open questions section captures unresolved items

## Content depth

For each topic, verify:

- [ ] Specific numbers, dates, and dollar amounts preserved
- [ ] Key decisions include the reasoning behind them
- [ ] Disagreements captured with both sides and their reasoning
- [ ] Trade-offs and rejected alternatives noted
- [ ] Constraints and scope boundaries mentioned

## Attribution

- [ ] Proposals attributed to the person who made them
- [ ] Reactions and follow-up questions attributed correctly
- [ ] Uncertain attributions flagged (e.g., "one participant noted...")
- [ ] Speaker labels from transcript resolved to real names where possible

## Action items audit

Cross-reference with transcript for:

- [ ] Explicit commitments ("I will...", "Let me take that...")
- [ ] Task assignments ("Can you handle...", confirmed by the person)
- [ ] Deferred items ("Let's revisit...", "Phase 2...", "Not now...")
- [ ] Follow-ups needed ("Discuss offline...", "Check with...")

## Common omissions

Things frequently missed on first pass:

- Entity names, field names, technical terms mentioned in passing
- Numerical ranges, counts, and priorities
- Conditions and contingencies ("but only if...", "assuming that...")
- What the counterparty volunteered *unprompted* (high-signal, easy to miss)
- Moments of disengagement or topic changes by the counterparty
- Items explicitly deferred to future meetings or phases

## Red flags

Signs the draft may be incomplete:

- A long transcript section summarized in one sentence
- A decision recorded without reasoning
- An action item without a clear owner
- A topic the transcript spent significant time on that's absent from notes
